Origin Story

During the festive holidays of 2020, I (Your Boy, Buya) was tasked with the important mission of finding the perfect gift for my uncle, my mum's big bro. Being the creative genius that I am, I suggested creating a custom digital artwork for him. I dove headfirst into the project, determined to create something truly spectacular.
After weeks of tireless experimentation, I emerged victorious with a masterpiece, that brought immense joy to my uncle and family. And let me tell you, that feeling was addictive. That's why, with the help of my new partners, we're dedicated to spreading that joy as far and wide as we can.

Frequently Asked Questions

The completion time for a portrait depends on the size and complexity of the artwork and the number of revisions requested. Typically, we can complete an A3 and A2 portrait within a week, and an A1 portrait within 1-2 weeks.
However, please note that these are estimates and may vary depending on our workload and any additional requests or revisions. We will always keep you informed of the progress of your portrait and let you know if there are any delays.

For an A3 portrait, we recommend sharing 1-3 photos,
while 1-5 photos are recommended for an A2 portrait,
and 1-8 photos for an A1 portrait.

However, there are no hard and fast rules, and we are happy to work with whatever photos you provide.

We recommend using a high-quality photo with good lighting to ensure the best possible outcome for your portrait. However, in some cases, we may be able to work with lower-quality photos. Just send us the photo, and we'll let you know if we can use it to create your portrait. Keep in mind that the quality of the photo may affect the final result of the portrait and that we may need to make some adjustments or revisions to achieve the desired outcome.

To ensure the best possible outcome for your portrait, we recommend using:
- Good quality taken with a smartphone camera or better.
When sending your photos, we advise using email to maintain the best quality.
- Well-lit images can also make a big difference in the final quality of the portrait.

- If the photos are hardcopy, you can still scan them under good lighting, and still email them.
Alternatively, they can be delivered to us and returned with the portrait.
