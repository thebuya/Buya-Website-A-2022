The SB7 Framework

I look outside my room window, noticing the ever expanding flower I got with Sheila during `the Covid.` (She says it’s a different one, I beg to differ). The psychedelic is now kicking in, as I make a failed attempt at casting my mind back to a childhood memory. I know I have experienced this memory before yet I have no recollection of the location, people I was with, or more importantly Why I was there. I begin to go through a series of questions; Is the person I was then still me ? Will this current memory also be of a person I can't remember in the future ?. Is the world I lived in then, the same as the world I live in today ?. "Should I Care ?", I wonder.

What happened to all these foggy characters I can’t seem to remember ? Was one of them my Dad ? How do these characters (if still alive) look back on that moment? Can they remember me ? What happens when current me is now past me ? Will I still look back in the same way, not remembering where I was, or who I was with, or more importantly Why I Was anywhere. Will I look back with the feeling of “Oh, when you thought the world was yours ?” Or will it be a completely unique feeling, one I am yet to ever experience.

" The Future is a prison, and we are both the wardens and prisoners." Me, Myself & I

At that moment I begun to realise how often I have thought of my view as the “main view” in most memories. Yet, at this point I was now thinking of myself as a bystander in time. I am not the central figure of the story. The entire World Is, and I am a passenger. The story is the world.

This by stander feeling felt so important yet so ‘sad’ . I am just like everyone else, just another character in a story so old, we can’t all seem to agree when or how it started. Should I be sad about the fact that I am like everyone? Or should I be glad that none of my worries or problems matter at all since I am not central to the only thing that matters, This thing we call The Whole World.
