Haunted By The Future.

I look out my room window, noticing the ever-expanding flower I got with Sheila during the Covid Pandemic (she says it's a different one, but I beg to differ). The psychedelic is now kicking in, as I make a failed attempt to cast my mind back to a childhood memory. I know I have experienced this memory before, yet I have no recollection of the location, the people I was with, or, more importantly, why I was there. I begin to go through a series of questions: Is the person I was then still me? Will this current memory also be of a person I can't remember in the future ? Is the world I lived in then the same as the world I live in today? "Should I care?", I wonder.

What happened to all these foggy characters that I can't seem to remember? Was one of them my Dad? How do these characters, if still alive, look back on that moment? Can they remember me? What happens when the current me becomes the past me? Will I still look back in the same way, not remembering where I was, or who I was with, or more importantly why I was anywhere? Will I look back with the feeling of "Oh, when you thought the world was yours?" Or will it be a completely unique feeling, one I am yet to ever experience.

" The Future is a prison, and we are both the wardens and prisoners." Me, Myself & I

At that moment I begun to realise how often I have thought of my view as the “main view” in most memories. Yet, at this point I was now thinking of myself as a bystander in time. I am not the central figure of the story; the entire World Is, and I am a passenger. The story is the world.

This bystander felt so important yet so sad. I am just like everyone else, just another character in a story so old that we can't seem to agree on when or how it started. Should I be sad about the fact that I am like everyone else, or should I be glad that none of my worries or problems matter, after all I am not central to the only thing that matters: this thing we call the whole world?
