Who Not How

Are you not tired of trying to do everything yourself and feeling like you're always running in circles? üèÉüèæ

If you, like over 76% of the population, are seeking an efficient way to balance your ever-increasing workload, then you need to get your hands on 'Who Not How'! This revolutionary book, will transform the way you approach problem-solving and goal-setting.

The key idea of the book is to stop asking yourself, "How can I solve this problem?" and start asking, "Who can help me solve this problem?"! By finding the right people to help you achieve your goals, you can save time, energy, and money while achieving better results.
Plus, working with others can be more fun and rewarding than going it alone!

'Who Not How' is a treasure trove of practical advice, inspiring stories, and humorous anecdotes that will guide you in creating a network of talented individuals to help you achieve your goals.

For instance,the book emphasizes the importance of delegating tasks to experts in their respective fields. This allows you to free up your time and focus on what you do best. The book also provides tips on how to identify the right people to work with, and how to build strong relationships with them.

One inspiring story in the book is about a young entrepreneur who was struggling to grow her business. After realizing that she couldn't do it all alone, she sought the help of a mentor who had experience in her industry. Through their partnership, she was able to gain valuable insights and connections that helped her business thrive.

Another example of the book's advice is to focus on your unique abilities and delegate tasks that are not aligned with your strengths. By doing so, you can maximize your productivity and impact. The book also emphasizes the importance of collaboration and how working with others can lead to breakthroughs and innovations.

So, what are you waiting for? Start asking "who" and begin building your dream team today! With 'Who Not How', you'll have all the guidance and inspiration you need to accomplish anything you set your mind to.

By the end of the book, if you're curious as to whether 'Who Not How' is for you, (it has major CEO vibes), pause and ask yourself, "Does this book align with the person I am becoming or aspire to be?"
